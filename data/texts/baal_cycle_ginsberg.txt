THE BAAL CYCLE (Ginsberg/ANET Translation)

TABLET I: THE DEFEAT OF YAM

The scribe is Atta, priest of Baal, chief of the priests,chief of the herdsman.
He presents the face of Baal: I sing of the Lord of the Earth.

Mightiest Baal hears and replies:
'Prince Yam is dead, the rule of Lotan is ended.
The mighty dragon with seven heads shall be destroyed.'

Kothar-wa-Hasis speaks:
'I tell you, O Prince Baal, I declare to you, O Rider of the Clouds:
Now your enemy, Baal, now your enemy you shall slay,
Now you shall cut off your foe.
You shall take your eternal kingdom, your dominion forever and ever.'

The cup in Baal's hand is taken,
The goblet in the hands of the Victor:
A cup for the gods to drink,
A goblet for the assembly of the Holy Ones.

TABLET II: BAAL'S HOUSE

Prince Baal says: 'I alone am he who rules over the gods,
Who fattens gods and men, who satisfies the multitudes of the earth.'
He cries to Kothar-wa-Hasis:
'Hurry, build a house for me, raise a palace for me in the heights.'

Kothar-wa-Hasis replies:
'I will build, O Prince Baal, I will raise a palace for you.
I will make a window in the house, a casement within the palace.'

Baal says: 'Let no window be made in the house, no casement within the palace.'
Kothar-wa-Hasis replies: 'You will understand, O Baal, what I mean.'

Baal's house is built of silver and gold,
A house of lapis-lazuli and cedar.
He slaughters oxen and sheep, bulls and fatlings,
He calls his brothers into his house, his kinsmen into his palace.

TABLET III: BAAL AND MOT

Now Mighty Baal goes to the mountain of the gods.
He sends messengers to Mot, the son of El:
'Message of Mighty Baal: Greetings to you, O Divine Mot.
I am your servant, I am your slave forever.'

Mot replies: 'I will consume Baal, I will make him like a lamb in my mouth,
Like a kid in my jaws I will crush him.'

Mot says: 'I invite Baal to my feast, to eat bread with me,
To drink wine at my table. Let him come to my mountain.'

Baal fears and goes down to Mot's realm.
He descends into the earth, he enters the underworld.
The gods weep for Baal, they pour ashes on their heads.

TABLET IV: THE SEARCH FOR BAAL

Anat, the sister of Baal, searches for him.
She seeks him on every mountain, in every valley.
She finds his body fallen to the ground.
She takes him up and buries him with honor.

Anat goes to El, the Father of Years:
'Give me a son, O El, that I may have a son like Baal.'
El grants her request, and a new warrior is born.

But Anat continues to seek vengeance:
She seizes Mot, she cuts him with a sword,
She winnows him with a sieve, she burns him with fire,
She grinds him with a millstone, she scatters him in the field.

TABLET V: BAAL'S RETURN

After seven years, Baal returns.
He challenges Mot: 'Mot, give me my brothers, give me my kinsmen.'
They fight like bulls in the dust.

Baal prevails and takes his throne again.
He opens the window in his palace,
He sends forth his voice in thunder,
The earth shakes at his voice.

He pours out rain and dew,
He makes the earth fruitful again.

TABLET VI: THE VICTORY OF BAAL

Baal sits enthroned in his house,
He rules over the gods from his mountain.
The gods bring tribute to Baal,
They acknowledge his kingship.

El blesses Baal: 'May Baal live and reign,
May he give rain in season,
May he make the fields fruitful.'

The cycle ends with praise to Baal:
'Baal lives! The Lord of the Earth reigns!'
