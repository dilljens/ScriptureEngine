# Isaiah

**Language**: Hebrew  **Verses**: 1,292  **Chapters**: 66

## Structure

```
Isaiah contains two major divisions with chiastic structure:

Judgment (1–39)
├── Oracles against Judah/Jerusalem (1–12)
│   ├── Temple vision (6)
│   └── Messianic kingdom (9, 11)
├── Oracles against nations (13–27)
├── Social oracles (28–35)
└── Historical interlude — Hezekiah (36–39)

Consolation (40–66)
├── Return from exile (40–48)
├── Servant Songs (49–55)
└── Future glory / new creation (56–66)
```

## Key Literary Patterns

### The Servant Songs
Four passages describe the "Servant of the Lord":
1. **Isaiah 42:1–9** — The Servant's mission
2. **Isaiah 49:1–13** — The Servant's call
3. **Isaiah 50:4–11** — The Servant's suffering
4. **Isaiah 52:13–53:12** — The Suffering Servant

### Chiastic Structure of Isaiah 6
The temple vision chapter forms a clear chiasm:
```
A — Divine vision (vv1–2)
  B — Response of creation (v3–4)
    C — Prophet's confession (v5)
      D — Purification (v6–7)
    C' — Prophet's commission (v8)
  B' — Divine message (v9–10)
A' — Judgment and remnant (v11–13)
```

## Intertextual Connections

Isaiah is the most quoted book in scripture:
- **NT**: 66 direct quotations — more than Psalms and Proverbs combined
  - Isa 6:9–10 quoted in all 4 Gospels + Acts
  - Isa 53 (Suffering Servant) quoted in Matthew 8, 1 Peter 2, Acts 8
  - Isa 61:1–2 quoted by Jesus in Luke 4:16–21
- **Book of Mormon**: Isaiah chapters 2–14 quoted in 2 Nephi 12–24 (nearly verbatim)
  - Isa 53 present in Mosiah 14–15
  - 1 Nephi 19–21 quotes Isa 48–49
  - 1 Nephi 22:1–2 is midrash on Isaiah
  - 2 Nephi 25:1–6 explains "plainness" of Isaiah's prophecies
  - Nephi's vision (1 Ne 11–14) is structurally parallel to Isaiah's temple vision
- **D&C**: Isa 11 referenced in D&C 113 (stem of Jesse)
  - Isa 29 quoted in D&C 4 (marvelous work)
  - Isa 52:8 referenced in D&C 128 (watchmen)

## Literary Devices

- **Parallelism**: Isaiah is Hebrew poetry — synonymous, antithetic, and synthetic parallelism throughout
- **Wordplay**: "Isaiah" (יְשַׁעְיָהוּ) = "YHWH is salvation" — the theme of the book
- **Inclusio**: Book opens with rebellion (1:2) and closes with new creation (66:22–24)
- **Prophetic Perfect**: Hebrew verbs often use completed tense for future events (so certain they're "already done")

## Textual Notes

- The Great Isaiah Scroll (1QIsa^a) from the Dead Sea Scrolls is virtually identical to the Masoretic Text, confirming remarkable textual stability over 2,000+ years
- LXX of Isaiah differs in some passages, with some important Messianic readings (e.g., Isa 7:14 — parthenos vs almah)
- The Book of Mormon quotes from the brass plates version of Isaiah, which sometimes agrees with LXX against MT
